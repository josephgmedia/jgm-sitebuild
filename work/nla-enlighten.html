The Brief

Large-scale architectural projection mapping for the National Library of Australia as part of Canberra's annual Enlighten Festival. The brief was to transform the library's neoclassical facade into a living canvas - celebrating the building's history through motion and light.

The Approach

Led design across multiple building surfaces, developing motion content that worked with the architecture rather than against it. Every column, cornice, and window became part of the composition. The challenge was scale - content had to read from 200 metres away while maintaining detail up close.

The show ran nightly across the festival, synced to an original music composition that drove the pacing and emotional arc of the visual narrative.
