Weightless — A State of Mind

The Brief

Sydney metal band Weightless approached me with a visual idea: a Rorschach ink blot on their signature branding — safety yellow. They wanted a cyberpunk type final artwork. This was enough for me to expand on and elevate once digging deeper into what the EP meant to them conceptually.

The Approach

Before sketching anything, I asked for a summary of each track of the EP to get a gauge on what the body of work represented. The band sent back a breakdown — alluding to each song mapping to a different aspect of mental health — and this gave me a way to honour the Rorschach concept they wanted and turn the artwork into a Rorschach of the EP itself.

I built the central artwork in 3D, mirrored it to create symmetry, and brought it into Photoshop to flatten the depth while still keeping 3D shading. The final resulted in every element pictured referencing a track — peeling skulls for Counterfeit, three heads on one body for Perspectives, an exploding skull revealing a grenade for Sabotage, ink drips for Bloodsucker, a cage trapping the mind for Mesmerised. Abstract enough to honour the original concept; specific enough that the artwork is the EP.

The package extended across the EP cover, back, inner sleeve and CD; an animated stage visualiser used for live shows; YouTube audio spectrum visualisers; static and animated "out now" and pre-save assets; plus four single covers with their own matching pre-save and out-now assets.
